# Biogeochemical Flows
**Planetary Boundary 5**

## Boundary Status

**Status**: Boundary transgressed  
**Control Variables**:  
- **Nitrogen**: Flow to oceans (currently 150 Tg N/yr; boundary: 62 Tg N/yr)  
- **Phosphorus**: Flow to oceans (currently 22 Tg P/yr; boundary: 11 Tg P/yr)

**Scientific Basis**: Excessive nitrogen (N) and phosphorus (P) from agriculture cause eutrophication, dead zones, and ecosystem collapse. Industrial nitrogen fixation (Haber-Bosch process) has doubled global N availability. Phosphorus mining is finite and non-renewable.

---

## Business Relevance

### Directly Impacted Sectors
- **Agriculture**: Fertilizer use (synthetic N, mined P)
- **Food Processing**: Nutrient losses in supply chain
- **Wastewater Treatment**: Nutrient discharge limits
- **Aquaculture**: Feed formulations, effluent management

### Environmental Impacts
- **Eutrophication**: Algal blooms, oxygen depletion, fish kills
- **Drinking Water Contamination**: Nitrate pollution
- **Coastal Dead Zones**: Hypoxic regions (e.g., Gulf of Mexico, Baltic Sea)
- **Atmospheric Pollution**: N₂O emissions (potent GHG; 265× CO₂ over 100 years)

## Recommended Metrics

### Nitrogen Flows
- **Fertilizer Application Rate**: kg N per hectare
- **Nitrogen Use Efficiency (NUE)**: Output N / Input N
- **N Surplus**: Applied N − Harvested N
- **N₂O Emissions**: Direct and indirect emissions (tCO₂e)

### Phosphorus Flows
- **P Application Rate**: kg P₂O₅ per hectare
- **P Use Efficiency (PUE)**: Output P / Input P
- **P Discharge**: kg P in effluent

### Effluent Quality
- **Total Nitrogen (TN)**: mg/L in wastewater discharge
- **Total Phosphorus (TP)**: mg/L in wastewater discharge
- **Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD)**: mg/L

## Mitigation Strategies

### Agricultural Practices
- **Precision Agriculture**: Variable rate fertilizer application
- **Cover Cropping**: Nutrient retention in soil
- **Crop Rotation**: Nitrogen-fixing legumes
- **Manure Management**: Reduce nutrient runoff

### Industrial & Wastewater
- **Nutrient Recovery**: Struvite precipitation (P recovery)
- **Advanced Treatment**: Biological nutrient removal (BNR)
- **Circular Economy**: Wastewater as fertilizer source
