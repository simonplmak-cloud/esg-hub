# About ESG Hub

**ESG Hub** is a comprehensive knowledge library for sustainability professionals, analysts, and decision-makers, providing curated access to authoritative ESG frameworks, standards, and resources.

## Mission

To democratize access to high-quality ESG knowledge by:

- Organizing international standards and frameworks in a coherent structure
- Providing direct links to primary source documents (no consultancy summaries)
- Integrating Hong Kong and Asia-Pacific regulatory guidance
- Connecting frameworks to Simon Mak's practical ESG book series

## Framework Architecture

ESG Hub is organized around three internationally recognized frameworks:

### Environmental (E)
**Framework**: 9 Planetary Boundaries (Stockholm Resilience Centre)

- Climate Change
- Biodiversity Loss
- Land-System Change
- Freshwater Use
- Biogeochemical Flows
- Ocean Acidification
- Atmospheric Aerosol Loading
- Stratospheric Ozone Depletion
- Novel Entities

### Social (S)
**Framework**: ISO 26000 — 6 Social Core Subjects

- Organizational Governance
- Human Rights
- Labour Practices
- Fair Operating Practices
- Consumer Issues
- Community Involvement & Development

### Governance (G)
**Framework**: G20/OECD Principles of Corporate Governance (2023)

- Effective CG Framework
- Shareholder Rights & Equitable Treatment
- Institutional Investors & Intermediaries
- Disclosure & Transparency
- Responsibilities of the Board
- Sustainability & Resilience

## Content Principles

**Primary Sources Only**

All linked documents come from:
- Standard-setting bodies (IFRS, GRI, ISSB, EFRAG)
- International organizations (UN, OECD, ILO, World Bank)
- Government regulators (HKEX, SEC, EU Commission)
- Scientific institutions (IPCC, IPBES, Stockholm Resilience Centre)
- Industry initiatives (SBTi, CDP, PCAF, TNFD)

**Zero consultancy firm content** — no PwC, EY, Deloitte, KPMG, McKinsey, or law firm summaries.
